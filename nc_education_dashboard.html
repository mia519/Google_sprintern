Methodology & caveats

  • Data vintages differ: CRDC discipline = 2017, CCD directory (enrollment/FRL) = 2022, achievement (SPG) = 2025. An 8-year spread between discipline and achievement data.
  • School-name joins use fuzzy matching (rapidfuzz, token_sort_ratio, threshold 87, exact county) except the CRDC→CCD join, which uses the exact NCES school ID (ncessch).
  • Alternative/special-ed/CTE schools excluded from all figures (school_type ≠ 1) to avoid tautological suspension-rate outliers.
  • Suspension rate → absenteeism has a likely mechanical component: out-of-school suspension days are recorded as absences in most attendance systems, so part of this relationship may be definitional rather than a fully independent causal channel. The achievement relationship doesn't share this overlap.
  • Title I flag: the source file only marks "Y" for participating schools with blanks otherwise (no explicit "N"); blanks are treated as non-Title-I here, a standard but unverified assumption.
  • Transit/transportation data is not included in this dashboard. A composite "transit desert score" and a raw transit-stop count were both evaluated in an earlier pass; the composite's weighting formula couldn't be verified and the raw count's relationship with outcomes wasn't robust enough to stand behind, so transit access is left out of this build entirely rather than presented with caveats attached. Vehicle access (ACS % no vehicle) was also evaluated in a prior session (r=0.13 bivariate, mostly washes out under FRL control) but that dataset wasn't available this session either.
  • Title I eligibility mechanics (for context): districts rank schools by poverty rate and a school qualifies for a "schoolwide" Title I program once ≥40% of its students are from low-income families (waiver to 35% in some states). FRL eligibility (SY2025-26): free meals at household income ≤130% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines, reduced-price 130-185%.
  • County names are derived from CCD's numeric county_code via the standard NC FIPS crosswalk (validated against transit dataset's independently-labeled county names, ~77% exact string agreement, consistent with expected fuzzy-match slack).
