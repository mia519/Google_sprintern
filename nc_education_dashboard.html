Fuzzy-match audit (25-pair random sample plus every boundary-score and zero-shared-token case inspected by hand): found and corrected 2 confirmed false-positive matches out of ~1,740-1,850 schools per join — Southwest Elementary (Guilford) had been matched to Southern Elementary's absenteeism rate, and Macon Middle School (Madison) had been matched to Madison Middle School's achievement score. Both were nulled out and the full battery was regenerated; every reported r-value moved by less than 0.001 as a result. The threshold (87) is precision-leaning: it excludes a handful of genuinely-correct "X Elementary" vs. "X Elementary School" pairs to keep false positives this rare.

Student-teacher ratio data-quality check: 5 schools had teachers_fte recorded as exactly 1.0 regardless of enrollment (6 to 1,961 students), producing physically implausible ratios (one as high as 1961:1) — almost certainly a missing-data placeholder in the source file. Found when the sample grew after removing the FRL-rate requirement (those 5 schools had happened to also be missing FRL rate, which had been incidentally filtering them out). All 5 were nulled before any statistic in this dashboard was computed.

Methodology & caveats

  • Poverty rate (FRL) and suspension rate are not in this analysis. Both were removed per explicit instruction — not hidden, actually dropped as factors and, for FRL rate, as the poverty-control covariate used in every other regression. That means the remaining full-battery factor (student-teacher ratio) is no longer poverty-adjusted: its "county fixed effects" check now only nets out county-level differences, with no control for school poverty. Read its relationships as raw/ county-adjusted associations, not poverty-independent effects.
  • Data vintages differ: CCD directory (enrollment/student-teacher ratio) = 2022, achievement and Title I (SPG) = 2025, graduation rate = 2025.
  • Graduation rate is a high-school-only metric: NC DPI's cohort graduation rate is only reported for schools with a graduating cohort, so only ~424-428 of the state's ~1,740-1,840 analyzed schools have a value (the standard 4-year cohort rate, "cgr_type=STD", subgroup=ALL; the file's 5-year extended-cohort rate is not used, to keep a single consistent definition). Treat graduation-rate findings as describing high schools specifically, not all NC public schools.
  • School-name joins use fuzzy matching (rapidfuzz, token_sort_ratio, threshold 87, exact county).
  • Alternative/special-ed/CTE schools excluded from all figures (school_type ≠ 1), since those schools take students for reasons (prior discipline, specialized needs) that would distort class-size and outcome comparisons — see the sensitivity check above for what changes when this restriction is lifted.
  • Student-teacher ratio data quality: 5 schools had teachers_fte recorded as exactly 1.0 regardless of enrollment (6 to 1,961 students) — a near-certain missing-data placeholder rather than a real staffing count. Their ratios were nulled before any statistic was computed (see verification card above).
  • Title I flag: the source file only marks "Y" for participating schools with blanks otherwise (no explicit "N"); blanks are treated as non-Title-I here, a standard but unverified assumption.
  • Transit/transportation data is not included in this dashboard. A composite "transit desert score" and a raw transit-stop count were both evaluated in an earlier pass; the composite's weighting formula couldn't be verified and the raw count's relationship with outcomes wasn't robust enough to stand behind, so transit access is left out of this build entirely rather than presented with caveats attached.
  • Title I eligibility mechanics (for context): districts rank schools by poverty rate and a school qualifies for a "schoolwide" Title I program once ≥40% of its students are from low-income families (waiver to 35% in some states).
  • County names are derived from CCD's numeric county_code via the standard NC FIPS crosswalk (validated against transit dataset's independently-labeled county names, ~77% exact string agreement, consistent with expected fuzzy-match slack).
